What Is the Best AC Brand for Florida Homes?

By Soligo Air · April 2026 · 7 min read

When customers ask us "what's the best AC brand?" we give them the same answer every time: it depends on your budget, your home, and how long you plan to stay. That said, some brands consistently outperform others in Florida's specific climate — and we have opinions based on years of installing and servicing all of them.

What Makes Florida Different

Florida's combination of high humidity, salt air near the coast, and year-round operation is harder on HVAC equipment than almost anywhere else in the US. The best AC for a home in Michigan might fail earlier than expected in Orlando. Look for brands that:

  • Use corrosion-resistant coatings on coils
  • Have high SEER2 ratings (efficiency in heat)
  • Include good dehumidification performance
  • Come with strong parts warranties

Top AC Brands for Central Florida

Carrier — Best Overall

Carrier consistently ranks among the top for reliability and efficiency. Their Infinity series includes some of the highest SEER2 ratings available, and their coil protection options make them a strong choice for Florida's humidity. Price range: mid-to-high.

Trane — Most Durable

Trane builds equipment that lasts. Their "built to a higher standard" reputation is well-earned — Trane systems are known for withstanding heavy use. Great choice for homeowners who want to install once and forget it. Price range: high.

Lennox — Best Efficiency

If lowering your electric bill is the priority, Lennox leads the pack. Their XC25 unit is one of the most efficient residential ACs on the market. Higher upfront cost, but pays back over time. Price range: high.

Rheem — Best Value Mid-Range

Rheem offers solid reliability at a more accessible price point. Their Prestige series competes with the premium brands at a lower cost. A good choice for homeowners who want quality without the premium price tag. Price range: mid.

Goodman — Best Budget Option

Goodman gets a bad reputation it doesn't fully deserve. With proper installation and regular maintenance, a Goodman system can serve a Florida home well for 10–15 years. Best for rental properties or budget-conscious buyers. Price range: low-to-mid.

The Most Important Factor: Installation Quality

Here's the truth: the brand matters less than the installation. A premium Carrier system installed incorrectly will underperform a budget Goodman installed properly. Proper sizing (Manual J calculation), correct refrigerant charge, and sealed ductwork matter more than the brand name on the unit.

Choose a contractor you trust. Ask about their sizing process, warranty coverage, and what happens if something goes wrong after installation.
