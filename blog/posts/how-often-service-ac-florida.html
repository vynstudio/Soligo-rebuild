How Often Should You Service Your AC in Florida?

By Soligo Air · April 2026 · 5 min read

In most parts of the country, HVAC professionals recommend servicing your air conditioner once a year. In Florida, that advice doesn't go far enough.

Florida Is Harder on Your AC Than Most States

Your air conditioner in Orlando runs 9 to 11 months a year — compared to 3 to 5 months in northern states. That extra runtime adds up fast: more wear on the compressor, more moisture cycling through the system, more opportunities for the drain line to clog or the coils to ice up.

Central Florida's humidity alone makes AC maintenance more important. When moisture levels are consistently high, your system works harder to dehumidify the air — and components like the evaporator coil, drain pan, and blower motor take more stress as a result.

Our Recommendation: Twice a Year

For most Central Florida homeowners, we recommend two tune-ups per year:

  • Spring (March–April): Before the summer heat hits. This is the most important tune-up — catch problems before peak season when AC companies are busiest.
  • Fall (October–November): After the heavy summer run. Check for wear, clean up after months of intense use, and prep for the mild winter season.

What Happens During a Tune-Up?

A proper AC tune-up isn't just a quick look. At Soligo Air, our $89 flat-rate tune-up includes:

  • Refrigerant level check and pressure test
  • Condenser and evaporator coil inspection and cleaning
  • Drain line flush to prevent backups and water damage
  • Thermostat calibration
  • Electrical connection tightening
  • Blower motor and belt inspection
  • Full written report with any recommendations

The Cost of Skipping Maintenance

The average AC repair in Orlando costs $150–$600. A compressor replacement can run $1,200–$2,500. A $89 tune-up that catches a refrigerant leak early or clears a clogged drain line can prevent either of those outcomes.

Beyond repairs, a poorly maintained system is less efficient — meaning higher electric bills every month. Studies show that regular maintenance keeps your AC running at 95% of its original efficiency, while a neglected system can drop to 70–80% over time.
