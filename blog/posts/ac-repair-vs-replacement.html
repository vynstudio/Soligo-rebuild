AC Repair vs. Replacement: How to Know Which You Need

By Soligo Air · April 2026 · 6 min read

It's one of the most common questions we hear: "Should I repair my AC or just replace it?" The honest answer depends on several factors — and any HVAC company that gives you a quick answer without looking at your system first isn't doing you any favors.

The 5,000 Rule

One of the most useful frameworks for this decision is the $5,000 rule: multiply the age of your system (in years) by the repair cost. If the result is more than $5,000, replacement is usually the smarter financial move.

Example: Your 12-year-old AC needs a $500 repair. 12 × $500 = $6,000. That's over the threshold — replacement is worth considering.

Example 2: Your 5-year-old AC needs a $400 repair. 5 × $400 = $2,000. Well under the threshold — repair makes sense.

Clear Signs You Should Repair

  • Your system is less than 8 years old
  • The repair is a single, straightforward issue (capacitor, contactor, refrigerant top-off)
  • The repair cost is less than 30% of replacement cost
  • Your system has been well-maintained and running efficiently
  • You haven't had repeated breakdowns in the past 2 years

Clear Signs You Should Replace

  • Your system is 12+ years old (Florida systems age faster due to year-round use)
  • You're facing a compressor or coil replacement — expensive repairs on aging equipment
  • Your system uses R-22 refrigerant (Freon), which is no longer manufactured and very expensive
  • You've had multiple repairs in the past 2 years
  • Your electric bills keep rising despite regular maintenance
  • Your home never reaches a comfortable temperature even when the AC runs constantly

The Cost Comparison for Orlando Homeowners

Typical AC repair costs in Central Florida: $150–$800 for most common issues. Compressor replacement: $1,200–$2,500.

A new system (equipment + installation): $4,000–$8,000 depending on size and efficiency rating. With 0% financing options, a new system can cost as little as $110–$220/month over 36 months.

New systems are also significantly more efficient — a 16 SEER unit vs. an older 10 SEER system can cut your cooling costs by 30–40%.
